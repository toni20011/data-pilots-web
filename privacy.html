Privacy & Cookie Policy

Last updated: April 2026  ·  Data Pilots  ·  datapilots.tech

We built Data Pilots to help businesses be more transparent about data. We hold ourselves to the same standard. This policy tells you exactly what we collect, why, and what we do with it — in plain English.

1. Who we are

Data Pilots is operated by Toni Dumas, trading as Data Pilots (datapilots.tech). We are the data controller for any personal data we hold about you.

If you have any questions about this policy or want to exercise your rights, contact us at team@datapilots.tech.

2. What we collect and why

When you run a free Trust Check

You submit a website URL. That URL is passed to our scan engine, which analyses publicly visible content on that website.

We do not store your results on our servers after your session ends. Parsed scan results are temporarily stored in your own browser (localStorage) for up to 24 hours so you can return to them or attach them to a new account without re-scanning. This storage is local to your device — it is not accessible to us.

No personal data is collected from you in order to run a free scan. No account, no email, no name.

When you contact us

If you email us or book a call via Calendly, we receive your email address and the content of your message. We use this to respond to you. We do not add you to any mailing list without your explicit agreement.

When you create a paid account (Co-Pilot)

We collect your email address and store your scan results so you can track progress over time. Paid scans retain their Papaya consent session so you can access the full audit detail. You can view, export, or delete your results at any time by contacting us at team@datapilots.tech.

If you save a free scan to your new account at sign-up, that result is stored under your account from that point forward and subject to the same retention terms as a paid scan.

What we do not collect

  • We do not run analytics tools (no Google Analytics, no tracking pixels)
  • We do not use advertising or retargeting cookies
  • We do not collect data from the websites you ask us to scan beyond what is publicly visible
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties for marketing purposes

3. Cookies

We use no non-essential cookies. No analytics. No advertising. No tracking.

The only cookie that may be set is __cf_bm, placed by Cloudflare (our hosting provider) for bot protection. This is a strictly necessary technical cookie that does not track you or build any profile. Under UK PECR, strictly necessary cookies do not require your consent.

CookieSet byPurposeDuration
__cf_bm Cloudflare Bot management / security — distinguishes humans from automated traffic 30 minutes

We review our cookie use whenever we add new features. If we ever introduce non-essential cookies, we will update this policy and add a consent mechanism before doing so.

4. How we use AI

Our AI principles

  • We always tell you when AI is involved. The Trust Check uses an AI browser agent (Papaya) to audit your site. This is disclosed on the product and in these pages.
  • AI findings are for human review, not automated decisions. Results are presented for you to interpret and act on. No automated decision is made about your business.
  • We use AI only for what we say. Papaya is used exclusively to audit publicly visible website content for trust and compliance signals. We do not use scan data to train models or build profiles of any kind.
  • Data minimisation. Only the URL you submit is sent to Papaya. No personal data from your site is extracted or stored.

About Papaya

Papaya is the AI browser agent we use to run live consent audits. When you submit a URL, Papaya visits that URL and analyses what is publicly visible — the same way a real buyer or GDPR auditor would. It checks cookie banners, consent behaviour, and policy links.

What Papaya doesWhat Papaya does not do
Visits the URL you submit Collect personal data from the site being scanned
Analyses publicly visible content Retain a copy of the scan after we have parsed the results
Returns findings to Data Pilots Associate results with any personal identifier

Deletion is automatic and immediate. As soon as Papaya returns results and we have parsed them, we send a deletion request to Papaya's API — their copy is removed without any action required from you. We do not wait for a timer or a user event. On a free scan, Data Pilots also discards the results when your session ends. On a paid account, results are saved to your account only, and you can delete them at any time.

6. Legal basis for processing (GDPR)

We process personal data under the following legal bases:

  • Legitimate interests — responding to enquiries, running the scan service, keeping the site secure
  • Contract — delivering the paid Co-Pilot service to customers who have signed up
  • Consent — only where explicitly collected (e.g. if we ever add a newsletter)

7. How long we keep your data

  • Free scan results: not stored — deleted at end of session
  • Email enquiries: kept for as long as the conversation is active, then deleted within 12 months
  • Paid account data: kept for as long as your account is active, then deleted within 30 days of account closure

8. Your rights

Under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate data
  • Delete your data (right to erasure)
  • Object to how we use your data
  • Portability — receive your data in a portable format
  • Withdraw consent at any time where consent is the basis for processing

To exercise any of these rights, email team@datapilots.tech. We will respond within 30 days.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO): ico.org.uk

9. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes — for example, if we introduce analytics or a new third-party tool — we will update this page and change the "Last updated" date at the top. We will not use your data in ways that contradict what was in place when you provided it without giving you the chance to opt out first.
