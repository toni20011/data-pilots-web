# 01 — Strategy

## Positioning

**The Trust Check for online training businesses selling to corporate clients.**

The Trust Check IS the risk assessment — it's not two separate things. The Trust Check surfaces where a business is breaking trust and creating compliance issues.

Cleaner framing (from advisor call, April 2026):
> "The Trust Check — your risk assessment to see where you could be breaking trust and creating compliance issues."

Core value in one sentence:
> "We help L&D businesses see and fix the risks that stop clients from trusting them — before those risks cost them a deal."

**Two-part value:**
1. **See** — risk assessment of the public-facing website (and eventually portal/platform) across UX, transparency, safety, and AI trust
2. **Act** — prioritised, plain-language guidance to fix what matters most, without needing a lawyer or developer

---

## Audience

**Primary target:** Solo L&D practitioners and small L&D companies selling B2B into corporate/enterprise clients.

**Profile:**
- Strong at their core craft (learning design, facilitation, content)
- Not data/compliance experts — rely on plain language
- Typically 1–20 people
- Selling into HR, L&D, or procurement teams at mid-to-large employers
- Often losing deals they don't understand why they lost

**Buyer personas (the people who check them):**

| Persona | Primary concern |
|---|---|
| Enterprise L&D buyer | Supplier credibility, learner data handling |
| HR buyer | GDPR, consent, who can access learner data |
| IT / Security | Security headers, CSP, HTTPS |
| Legal / Procurement | DPA, privacy policy coverage, terms |

---

## Differentiation

Most compliance tools are built for legal teams or developers. Data Pilots is built for business owners who are neither.

| What others do | What Data Pilots does |
|---|---|
| Legal jargon | Plain language |
| One-time audit report | Ongoing, team-trackable progress |
| Technical findings only | Business impact + fix guide + time-to-fix |
| Generic compliance checklists | Mapped to the specific context of L&D/corporate sales |
| Done-for-you or do-it-yourself | Give you the picture AND give your team the path |

The core IP is the **translation layer** between expert/compliance frameworks (GDPR Art. 13, ISO 27001, SOC 2) and plain business language — framed through the lens of what corporate buyers actually check.

## Go-to-market approach

**Acquisition:**
- Free Trust Snapshot — zero friction, immediate value, no account required
- Hook: "See your score in 5 minutes"
- Conversion: results show gaps → community join → paid Panel

**Community as flywheel:**
- Community members are the product's first users, beta testers, and case studies
- Direct input shapes what gets built
- Word of mouth within L&D professional communities

**Content / trust signals:**
- Resource hub: practical guides written for the audience
- Framework page: explains the methodology (builds credibility with procurement-aware buyers)
- Founder story: Toni's credibility — "built by people who've been on both sides"

---

## "Now what?" problem

From advisor call (April 2026): people don't know what to do with insights once they have them. **Findings alone are not enough — action guidance is the differentiator.**

Every finding in Data Pilots should be paired with:
1. Why it matters (business impact + compliance reference)
2. What to fix (plain-language fix guide)
3. Who on the team should own it
4. How long it will take (effort + time-to-fix)
