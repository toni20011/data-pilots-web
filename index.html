Got questions?

Why Data Pilots — and not just ChatGPT or a free scanner?

Fair question. Here's the honest answer.

Can't I just ask ChatGPT to check my website?

You can — and you'll get a generic checklist back. What you won't get is a real assessment that actually visits your site, fires cookies, clicks "Decline All," and checks whether tracking actually stops. ChatGPT doesn't visit URLs. It guesses based on training data.

Our Trust Check works differently. It combines three things: a live site walkthrough that visits your website the way a real buyer or auditor would; direct analysis of your website's signals — policy language, security headers, AI disclosure, and more; and a few short questions about your business and clients, because context changes what findings actually mean.

Everything gets combined and translated into plain English — prioritised by what matters most for the clients you're trying to win, with clear guidance for every person in your business who needs to act on it.

There are free cookie scanners out there. Why pay?

Free scanners check one thing: cookies. We check your whole trust picture — security headers, privacy policy quality, GDPR language, AI disclosure, footer policy links, consent banner behaviour, and how all of it lands with the specific type of buyer you're trying to win.

More importantly: free tools give you a list. We give you a prioritised action plan in plain English, with the context of why each issue matters to an L&D procurement team. That's the bit that turns findings into revenue.

Why do I need this specific to L&D? Trust is trust, isn't it?

Not quite. Enterprise L&D procurement has its own patterns: HR and L&D buyers care about data handling for learner records; legal teams ask about DPAs and GDPR compliance; IT security checks your headers and cookie consent. A generic trust tool won't know that "no AI disclosure" is a bigger deal for an L&D buyer than for a florist.

We've built the scoring and action guidance around what actually blocks or accelerates L&D deals — so your results are relevant, not just technically accurate.

What do I actually get that I couldn't piece together myself?

Three things that are hard to replicate solo: (1) Both risk types in one place — most tools focus on UX or compliance. We identify both trust risks (what breaks buyer confidence) and compliance risks (what triggers a procurement veto) in a single scan. (2) Three perspectives, not one — findings are framed for your buyers and clients, for leadership (decisions without jargon), and for your legal or technical team (what they need to fix it). (3) Prioritised action plan — not a list of 40 issues, but the 3 things to fix this week that have the biggest impact.

You could spend half a day pulling this together across different tools, then try to translate technical output into something your team can act on. Or you can have it in 5 minutes — in language you can actually use.

What's the difference between the free check and the paid Co-Pilot?

The free Trust Check scans your public website — the face you show to buyers before they even speak to you. It covers the basics that get flagged in procurement: security, privacy docs, consent behaviour, transparency signals.

The paid Co-Pilot goes deeper: your learning portal, your platform integrations, your contracts and DPAs. It also includes human-led guidance, ongoing monitoring, and a Trust Check-In call where we walk through findings with your team and give you a clear action roadmap. It's the difference between a self-test and having an expert on your side.

How is this different from hiring a lawyer or a data consultant?

Lawyers and data consultants are essential for complex compliance work — we're not replacing them. What we do is surface the practical, fixable issues that stop deals before they even get to a legal review. Most of our top findings take 1–2 days to fix without any specialist help.

Think of it as triage: we tell you what's broken and how to fix it in plain English. If something needs a lawyer, we'll say so — but the majority of trust issues on L&D websites don't.

I'm not technical — will I understand the results?

That's exactly who we built this for. Every finding is written in plain language with a real-world example. If it says "Missing privacy page," we tell you what that means to a buyer, why it matters, and how to fix it — without needing a developer or a lawyer.

What happens to my data when I run a scan?

Nothing is stored unless you choose to save your results. We scan your public-facing website and return a score. No account needed for the free check. You are always in control of what gets saved.

Who is this built for?

L&D coaches, consultants, and small training firms selling to enterprise clients or navigating procurement processes. If your buyers check your website before agreeing to meet, this is for you. No technical background needed.

Can I share my trust report with prospects?

Yes — that's one of the most popular uses. Co-Pilot includes an exportable trust proof PDF designed to go into proposals and procurement responses. It shows buyers at a glance that you take data and privacy seriously.
