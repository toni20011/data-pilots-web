See a real example

A training company in California received a formal notice from the state Attorney General's office. A California resident had used the AG's online complaint tool to flag that the website had no privacy policy — something California law requires for any business that collects visitor data, including email signups. The company had 30 days to respond. Legal advice, policy writing, and the stress of the process cost far more than the fix would have.

What triggered it: No privacy policy visible on the site. California's CCPA gives residents the right to report this directly to the state AG.

Corporate clients don't just buy your work. They vet your business first. A procurement team running a vendor check finds the same gaps a regulator would. Once flagged, the door closes — usually without explanation.

See a real example

An executive coaching firm spent three months getting approval to run a paid pilot program with a mid-size US company. The HR director was ready. Budget was approved. Then the corporate IT and legal team ran their standard vendor review — they needed to inspect the platform where coaching notes and assessments would be stored. No data processing agreement on the website, no mention of how learner data was handled, third-party tools embedded with no disclosure. The pilot was "paused pending compliance review." It never restarted.

What triggered it: No DPA (Data Processing Agreement) available, learner portal not reviewed, data handling undocumented. Common blockers in corporate vendor approval processes.

Common questions

Straight answers.

No jargon. If something's still unclear, just ask us.

Is this for me?
Who is this built for?

Coaches, consultants, e-learning providers, and training businesses selling to corporate clients. If you're the founder or owner responsible for growth — and your buyers check your website before agreeing to meet — this is for you. No technical background required.

Can't I just use ChatGPT or a free scanner?

Free tools are a reasonable starting point — but they have a key limitation: they don't visit your site. ChatGPT works from training data, not a live crawl. Cookie scanners check one thing. Neither knows anything about your business, your clients, or what buyers in your market actually look for.

Our Trust Snapshot does a live walkthrough of your website — the same way a buyer or IT team would — then combines it with your business context. You get a scored picture across four pillars and plain-English priority fixes, not a generic checklist.

Why do I need something built for businesses like mine?

Because what matters depends on who's buying. Corporate buyers in your market run specific checks: IT teams look for security headers; legal teams want to see data agreements; HR and learning buyers care about how you handle people's records. A general trust tool won't know that a missing AI disclosure is a bigger issue for a training provider than it is for a florist.

We built the scoring and guidance around what actually blocks or accelerates deals for businesses like yours — selling online services and expertise to corporate clients. Your results are relevant, not just technically accurate.

I'm not technical — will I understand the results?

Yes, and that's the point. Every finding is written in plain language with a real-world example of what it means and what to do about it. If it says "Missing privacy page," we explain what a buyer actually sees, why it matters, and how to fix it — without needing a developer or lawyer to translate.

What do I get?
What do I actually get from the free Trust Snapshot?

Your free Trust Snapshot gives you:

  • A trust score out of 100 across four pillars — User Experience, Transparency, Safety & Security, and AI Trust
  • Your top three priority fixes, written in plain English — no jargon
  • Context on why each issue matters to the buyers you're selling to
  • Role-split guidance: what you need to do, what your developer needs to do, what your legal contact should handle

Trust Panel goes further — every finding (not just the top three), a review of your learner portal and client-facing tools, monthly re-scans, and a trust proof PDF for proposals.

What's the difference between the free Trust Snapshot and Trust Panel?

The Trust Snapshot audits your public-facing website — the first thing buyers see. It covers the signals that get flagged in procurement and gives you a score plus your top three fixes. No account needed, about five minutes.

Trust Panel runs a deeper public site audit (every finding, not just the top three), then extends the review to your learner portal, platform, and client-facing tools. You also get step-by-step fix guides for your whole team, a trust proof PDF for proposals, a progress dashboard, and monthly re-scans to stay green.

Can I share my trust report with prospects?

Yes — and that's one of the most common uses. Trust Panel includes a trust proof PDF designed to go straight into proposals and procurement responses. It shows buyers at a glance that you take data and privacy seriously, before they've even asked the question.

What does "Unlimited Guests" mean?

Guests are people you bring into your Trust Panel dashboard without needing a paid seat — your developer, a legal advisor, a contractor, an accountant. They can view findings and act on fix guides relevant to their role. Unlimited means exactly that: no per-seat charges for the people you need to loop in.

About Data Pilots
How is this different from hiring a lawyer or compliance consultant?

Lawyers and consultants are essential for complex compliance work — we're not replacing them. What we do is surface the practical, fixable issues that stop deals before they ever reach a legal review. Most of our top findings take 1–2 days to fix without specialist help.

Think of it as triage. We tell you what's broken and how to fix it in plain English. If something genuinely needs a specialist, we'll say so clearly.

What happens to my data when I run a scan?

We scan your public-facing website and return a score. Nothing is stored unless you choose to save your results. No account needed for the free Trust Snapshot. You're always in control of what gets saved.
